Franz Kafka

A surrealist author exploring alienation, absurdity, and existential dread.

Here is the timeline of Kafka's Life

  • 1883 - Franz Kafka was born on July 3, 1883, in Prague, Czech Republic (then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire), into a middle-class, German-speaking Jewish family.
  • 1893-1901 - Attended the Deutsche Knabenschule (German Boys' School). Later, he attended the rigorous Altstädter Deutsches Gymnasium (German High School) in Prague.
  • 1901-1906 - Enrolled at the German University in Prague in 1901. Initially studied chemistry but switched to law, which he completed with a doctorate in 1906.
  • 1906-1907 - Completed a year of unpaid legal clerkship, which was a requirement for law graduates.
  • 1908-1922 - Began working at the Workers Accident Insurance Institute in Prague in 1908, where he handled claims for work-related injuries. Concurrently, he wrote stories and essays in his spare time.
  • 1912 - Met Felice Bauer, who became his fiancée (on and off, with many letters exchanged). Wrote "The Judgment" and began work on "The Metamorphosis", two of his most famous works.
  • 1914-1917 - Ended his engagement to Felice in 1914, though they later reconciled. Suffered from anxiety and deteriorating health due to tuberculosis, which was diagnosed in 1917.
  • 1917-1923 - Struggled with worsening tuberculosis and retired from his insurance job in 1922. Continued writing, including "The Castle" and "The Trial", though both were unfinished at the time of his death.
  • 1923 - Moved to Berlin in hopes of escaping family tensions and worsening health. Lived with Dora Diamant, his final companion.
  • 1924 - Franz Kafka died on June 3, 1924, in Kierling, near Vienna, Austria, from complications related to tuberculosis at the age of 40.
